# Reasonable Adjustment Assertion

A template for asserting your right to reasonable adjustments under section 20 of the Equality Act 2010. This creates an evidential architecture — once this notice is sent, all communication must be in writing, everything is timestamped, everything is documented.

Based on the actual reasonable adjustment assertions used across all sixteen institutional fronts documented in the [Institutional Register](../INSTITUTIONAL_REGISTER.md).

Send this by email where possible. Record the date — the duty to make adjustments arises **immediately** upon notification.

---

## Template

```
Subject: Reasonable Adjustment Assertion — Equality Act 2010

Dear [RECIPIENT NAME OR "Sir/Madam"],

[INSTITUTION NAME]

NOTICE OF DISABILITY AND REQUEST FOR REASONABLE ADJUSTMENTS

I am writing to notify you that I am a disabled person within the meaning
of section 6 of the Equality Act 2010.

MY CONDITION
[DESCRIPTION OF YOUR DISABILITY OR CONDITION]

This condition has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on my
ability to carry out normal day-to-day activities, meeting the statutory
definition of disability under section 6(1) of the Equality Act 2010.

ADJUSTMENTS REQUIRED

Under section 20 of the Equality Act 2010, you are required to make
reasonable adjustments where a provision, criterion, or practice of
yours puts me at a substantial disadvantage compared to a non-disabled
person.

I require the following adjustments:

[SELECT AND KEEP THE ADJUSTMENTS THAT APPLY TO YOU. DELETE THE OTHERS.]

- All communication with me must be in writing (email or letter) only.
  No telephone calls.
- All correspondence must be sent in [large print / accessible format].
- Extended deadlines for any responses required from me.
- Advance written notice of any planned visits to my property, with a
  minimum of [NUMBER] working days' notice.
- No unannounced visits to my property.
- A named point of contact for all communication relating to my account.
- [ANY OTHER SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENT YOU REQUIRE]

SCOPE OF THIS ADJUSTMENT

These adjustments apply to ALL communication with me going forward — not
only to complaints or disputes, but to routine correspondence, account
management, enforcement, and any other contact.

This includes any third parties acting on your behalf, such as
enforcement agents, debt collectors, or field agents. You are responsible
for ensuring that any third party you instruct is aware of and complies
with these adjustments.

WHEN THIS DUTY ARISES

The duty to make reasonable adjustments arises immediately upon your
receipt of this notice. There is no implementation period. If you
telephone me, visit my property without written notice, or otherwise
fail to comply with these adjustments after receiving this letter, that
failure is itself an act of discrimination under section 21 of the
Equality Act 2010.

You do not need to wait for a medical assessment, occupational health
referral, or internal review before implementing these adjustments. The
duty is anticipatory and arises upon notification.

EVIDENTIAL PURPOSE

I request written confirmation that these adjustments have been recorded
on my account and will be applied to all future communication.

Please confirm in writing within 14 days of receipt.

Yours faithfully,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
```

## Placeholder Fields

| Placeholder | What to enter |
|-------------|---------------|
| `[YOUR NAME]` | Your full legal name |
| `[YOUR ADDRESS]` | Your current postal address |
| `[YOUR EMAIL]` | Your email address |
| `[INSTITUTION NAME]` | The organisation you are writing to |
| `[RECIPIENT NAME OR "Sir/Madam"]` | The name of the person handling your account, or "Sir/Madam" if unknown |
| `[DESCRIPTION OF YOUR DISABILITY OR CONDITION]` | A brief, factual description — e.g. "I have an autism spectrum condition which affects my ability to process verbal communication under pressure" or "I have a chronic pain condition which limits my mobility and ability to attend appointments at short notice" |
| `[NUMBER]` | The number of working days' notice you require before any visit |

---

## Why This Matters

The reasonable adjustment assertion does two things:

1. **It protects you** — the institution must accommodate your needs, and any failure to do so is actionable discrimination.

2. **It creates an evidential architecture** — by requiring everything in writing, you ensure that every communication is timestamped, documented, and available for disclosure. No more "we telephoned you on..." with no record. No more enforcement agents arriving without notice. Everything is on paper, everything is provable.

This is not a complaint. It is a legal notification that triggers an immediate, ongoing, and enforceable duty.

---

## Examples of Reasonable Adjustments

| Adjustment | Why it helps |
|------------|-------------|
| Email-only communication | Creates a written record of everything; avoids distressing phone calls |
| Large print correspondence | Ensures documents are accessible for visual impairments |
| Extended response deadlines | Provides time to process information and seek support |
| Advance notice of visits | Prevents distress from unannounced enforcement visits |
| Named point of contact | Avoids repeating your situation to different agents each time |
| No telephone contact | Prevents anxiety-inducing calls and ensures written records |
| Accessible formats | Documents in formats compatible with screen readers or other assistive technology |

## After Sending

- Record the date you sent the notice.
- If they do not confirm the adjustments within 14 days, follow up in writing referencing this notice.
- If they fail to comply with the adjustments — for example, by telephoning you or sending an enforcement agent without notice — that failure is evidence of discrimination under section 21 of the Equality Act 2010.
- Keep every response and every instance of non-compliance — it forms part of your evidential record.
- If adjustments are repeatedly ignored, see the [Ombudsman/Regulator Complaint](TEMPLATE_OMBUDSMAN_REFERRAL.md) template to escalate.
