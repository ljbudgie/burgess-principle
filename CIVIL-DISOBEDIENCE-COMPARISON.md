# Civil Disobedience Comparison

## Introduction
The Burgess Principle continues the tradition of civil disobedience, which challenges unjust laws and institutions through moral and physical resistance. However, it shifts from symbolic protest and collective action to proactive, operational legitimacy testing, declaring nullity through forensic evidence and immutable logging.

## Verdict
The Burgess Principle transforms civil disobedience from moral appeal and collective action into a universal, AI-bound, self-documenting legitimacy test that creates permanent public evidence of institutional default. It evolves protest into doctrine, enforcing sovereignty through forensic declaration rather than resistance alone.

Traditional civil disobedience resists unjust power. The Burgess Principle declares it NULL ab initio and logs the failure for all time.
