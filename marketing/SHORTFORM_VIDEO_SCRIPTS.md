# Short-Form Video Scripts & Ideas

Hi 👋

Here are some gentle, respectful short video ideas to help explain the Burgess Principle in a simple, human way.

Tone: Kind, empowering, easy to understand.  
Format: Vertical 9:16 (1080 × 1920) for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

### Video Idea 1 – "One Simple Question" (~30 seconds)

Hook (0-3s):  
"Have you ever felt like a system just didn’t see you as a real person?"

Main message (3-25s):  
Show calm visuals of everyday life (letters arriving, someone on their phone).  
Voiceover:  
"Before any big decision that affects you — a bill, a warrant, a credit decision — the Burgess Principle simply asks:  

‘Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* situation?’  

That one respectful question can make a real difference."

CTA (25-30s):  
"Try it yourself. The full toolkit is free on GitHub. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #BeSeen #KnowYourRights

### Video Idea 2 – "From Feeling Unseen to Being Heard" (~45 seconds)

Hook:  
"Many of us have felt invisible to big systems."

Story:  
Show someone receiving a confusing letter, then calmly typing on their phone.  
Voiceover:  
"Instead of fighting, you can politely ask for human attention using the Burgess Principle.  

It works with your existing rights — like reasonable adjustments under the Equality Act if you’re autistic and prefer email-only contact.  

Real people are already using it to get fairer treatment."

CTA:  
"The templates are free and easy to customise with any AI. Drop the repo in and try it. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #EqualityAct #HumanFirst

### Video Idea 3 – "Fork It & Share It" (~20 seconds)

Hook:  
"This might be one of the most useful things you can do on your phone today."

Visuals:  
Screen recording of someone forking the repo, then using the templates.  
Voiceover:  
"The Burgess Principle is completely free and open. Fork the repo, drop it into any AI, and get personalised letters that help you ask to be seen as a real person."

CTA:  
"Fork it. Share it. Help someone else feel seen too. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #OpenSource #HelpfulTools

### Production Tips
- Keep the tone calm and hopeful — no anger or urgency.
- Use clear, large captions.
- Music: gentle, uplifting, royalty-free tracks.
- Always include the GitHub link and #BurgessPrinciple.

Feel free to adapt these scripts however you like. The goal is simply to help more people discover a kinder, more human way to ask institutions to pay proper attention to their individual situation.
