# Template: Music Copyright Dispute – Burgess Principle Request for Human Review

Use this when dealing with wrongful Content ID claims, blocked monetisation, incorrect royalty allocation, or automated rejection of your music.

---

**Subject:** Request for Human Review – [Platform / Service] Music Copyright Decision Regarding My Track “[Track Title]”

Dear [Platform Name] Copyright / Content ID / Legal Team,

I am writing regarding the decision concerning my track “[Track Title]” (ISRC: [ISRC if known], uploaded [date], URL: [link]).

I respectfully ask for written confirmation on this single binary question in line with the Burgess Principle:

**“Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case before any decision was reached?”**

The specific facts of my case are:

• The track is an original composition / sound recording created by me [or co-created with…].
• Any samples/interpolations used have been properly cleared [or this is 100% original work].
• The automated match appears to be an error because [brief factual reason, e.g. “the melody is substantially different”, “the fingerprint match is false positive”, “I own the copyright”].
• This decision is causing ongoing harm: [monetisation blocked, track muted, revenue withheld, etc.].

I am not challenging copyright law itself. I am simply requesting that a real human reviewer with musical and legal judgment has personally examined the specific facts of my track and claim, rather than relying solely on automated fingerprinting or checklists.

I would be grateful for a response within 7 days confirming:

1. That a named human reviewer has reviewed my specific case.
2. The outcome of that review and any action taken (removal of claim, restoration of monetisation, correction of royalty allocation, etc.).

Thank you for treating my work as an individual creative piece rather than just another automated match.

Yours sincerely,  
[Your Full Name / Artist Name]  
[Contact details]

---

### Additional Tips for Music Use
- Attach evidence: waveform comparison, stems, creation dates, copyright registration (if you have one), or a simple “this is 100% original” declaration.
- Follow up with a DSAR if the platform is in the UK/EU (ask for records of how the automated decision was made and who reviewed it).
- For repeated issues, reference the pattern in a second letter and consider escalating to the platform’s regulator or small claims court.
