# TIMELINE — Burgess Principle

A chronological record of key events, from the originating incident in December 2025 through the v1.0.0 release on 18 April 2026. This serves as the historical record and evidence trail.

- **Framework version:** v1.0.0 (released 18 April 2026)
- **Author:** Lewis James Burgess, Darlington, UK
- **Canonical source:** [github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle](https://github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle)
- **Last updated:** 18 April 2026

---

## December 2025 — origin

- **E.ON forced entry.** Forced entry into the author's home under an unsigned warrant, leading to a smart meter dispute. This is the originating incident from which the Burgess Principle was developed.

## January 2026 — formalisation

- The binary test is formalised: *"Was the individual considered as an individual human being, or were they processed as a unit within a system?"*
- Statutory mapping begun against UK GDPR Article 22, Equality Act 2010 ss. 20 and 29, contract law, FOIA 2000, and consumer protection law.

## February 2026 — case files opened

- **Energy Ombudsman case EG021819-26** opened against E.ON (unsigned warrant, forced entry, smart meter dispute, £129M claim).
- **Energy Ombudsman case EG037844-26** opened against British Gas (frozen meter, zero consumption, £425 warrant charge).
- **Local Government Ombudsman case 26000967** opened against Darlington Borough Council on five grounds, including bulk PCN processing, disputed council tax, and Equality Act 2010 ss. 20 / 29 failures.

## March 2026 — enforcement and regulators

- **Equita** — six DBC enforcement cases identified, totalling **£3,413.82**. SAR submitted and partially fulfilled; liability orders requested but not provided.
- **EHRC** complaint filed naming **E.ON** and the **Energy Ombudsman**.
- **Ofgem** scheme complaint filed.
- **Six FOI requests** issued under FOIA 2000 to FSA, DWP, DfE, MoJ, Home Office, CQC, and FCA, framing the binary test as a process question.

## Early April 2026 — court-side confirmation and resolution

- **Andrew Fletcher**, Justices' Legal Adviser at Birmingham Magistrates' Court, provides **written confirmation of bulk warrant processing**.
- **Wave Utilities** becomes the **first fully resolved case** under the framework: both accounts brought to **£0.00**. This is held out as proof of concept.

## IP track (parallel)

- **UK Certification Mark UK00004343685** filed — Classes 41, 42, 45 — and entered examination at the UK IPO. Examiner: **Lisa Skeggs**.
- **USPTO filing** initiated via a US-licensed attorney; in progress.

## 18 April 2026 — release day

A single day on which the framework was released, adopted, and applied at scale.

- **v1.0.0 released.** The Burgess Principle is published as a stable, citable standard and ceases to be described as in development.
- **PR #68692** submitted to [`openclaw/openclaw`](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw) (73.3k forks). OpenClaw **adopts** the Burgess Principle as its governance framework. The PR is additive — no existing code modified.
- **Endorsement by Elon Musk** of OpenClaw as the **gateway to the X API**, on the same day as the OpenClaw adoption.
- **PR #12265** submitted to [`NousResearch/hermes-agent`](https://github.com/NousResearch/hermes-agent) (99.1k stars) proposing Burgess Principle integration. Additive — no existing code modified.
- **UK GDPR Article 22 challenges** sent to **Amazon** and **Disney+** challenging ad insertion in paid subscriptions.
- **xAI follow-up** sent in connection with the OpenClaw / X API gateway position.
