# Equality Act Starter Pack  
**— Using the Burgess Principle with Reasonable Adjustments**

Hi 👋

This short guide is for anyone who finds certain types of communication difficult — especially autistic people or those who experience sensory overload, phone anxiety, or processing challenges.

Under the **UK Equality Act 2010**, autism is recognised as a disability. You have a legal right to ask for **reasonable adjustments** so that services treat you fairly. You do **not** need a formal medical diagnosis. The law focuses on the impact your autism has on day-to-day life, not on paperwork.

### One Simple Way to Ask
Combine your right to reasonable adjustments with the Burgess Principle question:

**“Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* individual situation?”**

This gentle combination often gets better results because it asks for human attention while clearly stating your legal rights.

### Ready-to-Use Email Template (Email-Only Request)

**Subject:** Request for Reasonable Adjustments & Confirmation of Human Review

Dear [Team / Customer Services / Equality Lead],

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing as an autistic person. Autism is recognised as a disability under the UK Equality Act 2010, and I would like to request some simple reasonable adjustments to make communication easier for me.

My preferred adjustment is **email contact only**. This helps me process information clearly and reduces anxiety or sensory overload that can come with phone calls.

In addition, following the simple guidance in the Burgess Principle, I’d like to kindly ask:

**Was a human member of your team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* individual situation before any decision or action was taken?**

If someone did look at my specific circumstances, I’d really appreciate a short written note with their name or reference so I know my case received proper human care.

I’m happy to provide any further details that would help.

Thank you for understanding my needs and for treating this as the individual matter it is. I look forward to hearing back by [suggest a reasonable date, e.g. 14 days from now].

Yours sincerely,  
[Your Full Name]
