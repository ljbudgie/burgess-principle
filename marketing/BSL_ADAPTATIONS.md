# BSL & British Sign Language Adaptations  
**— Using the Burgess Principle with Sign Language Users**

Hi 👋

Many deaf people use British Sign Language (BSL) as their first or preferred language. The Burgess Principle and Equality Act reasonable adjustments can still be very helpful, but we need to adapt the communication methods.

### Key Reasonable Adjustments for BSL Users
Under the Equality Act 2010, organisations have a duty to make reasonable adjustments for deaf BSL users. Common adjustments include:

- Providing a BSL interpreter (in-person or remote via video call)
- Accepting video messages in BSL instead of written emails
- Allowing extra time for communication
- Using relay services or text-to-speech / speech-to-text tools
- Email or text as a backup when video isn’t possible

### Adapted Template for BSL Users

**Subject:** Request for Reasonable Adjustments (BSL User) + Human Review

Dear [Team / Customer Services / Equality Lead],

I hope this message finds you well.

I am a deaf BSL user. British Sign Language is my first language, and I face significant barriers with written English or phone calls.

Under the Equality Act 2010, I am requesting reasonable adjustments for communication. My preferred methods are:
- Video calls with a BSL interpreter, or
- Video messages in BSL (sent via email or WhatsApp), or
- Email/text as a backup if video isn’t possible.

In addition, following the Burgess Principle, I’d like to kindly ask:

**Was a human member of your team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* individual situation before any decision or action was taken?**

I would really appreciate a short written or video response confirming that my case has received proper human attention.

I’m happy to provide more details or clarify my communication preferences.

Thank you for understanding and for treating this as the individual matter it is.

Best regards,  
[Your Full Name]
