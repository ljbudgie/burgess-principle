# Case Study: E.ON Next — Forced Entry Under an Unsigned Warrant Without Individual Judicial Review

**Case Reference:** Warrant Application 11160-544079 (RE99022)  
**Sector:** Energy  
**Status:** Ongoing — Energy Ombudsman investigation active  
**Date of Incident:** 25 May 2025  
**Outcome:** Settlement reached with E.ON. Ombudsman ruling due by 6 May 2026.

---

## Summary

On 25 May 2025, E.ON Next executed forced entry at a residential property under warrant Application 11160-544079, issued by Birmingham Magistrates' Court on 16 May 2025. The warrant was unsigned on its face — three magistrates' names were discovered embedded in the PDF metadata only, not visible on the document. The complainant has bilateral sensorineural hearing loss. E.ON's own field notes recorded "no contact psr's on account Cust is deaf," yet enforcement proceeded without any reasonable adjustment for communication. When the Burgess Principle question was asked — *was a human mind with proper authority individually applied to the specific facts of this case before the forced entry was authorised?* — the answer was no. The warrant was bulk-processed via CSV batch application with en bloc granting.

## Doctrinal Analysis

This case demonstrates the Burgess Principle applied at the most serious end of automated enforcement — forced entry to a person's home.

**NULL identified.** The warrant was issued through a bulk automated process. Birmingham Magistrates' Court processed energy warrant applications via CSV batch with en bloc granting. Only 5 out of every 100 applications were individually sampled. The remaining 95% — including this one — were granted without any named magistrate reviewing the specific facts of the specific case. The warrant was unsigned on its face. 536,139 energy warrants were processed through the same architecture in 18 months.

**Consequence of NULL.** A locksmith drilled the rear patio door locks of a deaf person's home while they were at work. The occupant returned to find their home had been entered. Multiple further visits followed. The system knew the occupant was deaf — E.ON's own field notes recorded it — yet no adjustment was ever made.

**The question.** When the complainant asked *"who individually reviewed this warrant before my home was entered?"* — no individual could be named. The warrant was bulk-processed. The court dip-sampled. The energy company printed the warrant and sent the enforcement team. Nobody checked whether the person in this home, with these circumstances, should have been subject to forced entry on this day.

**The contrast.** One question — asked calmly, in writing — exposed a systemic process affecting hundreds of thousands of warrants. The principle did not require legal training or confrontation. It required one question and a willingness to read the warrant that the system assumed nobody would.

## Lesson

This case shows what happens when enforcement at the most intrusive level — forced entry to a home — is processed without individual human review. A deaf person's locks were drilled under an unsigned warrant that was batch-processed alongside thousands of others. Nobody checked the specific facts. Nobody reviewed the specific circumstances. Nobody saw the person.

One calm question exposed the defect. The principle works at every scale — from a water bill to a warrant. Ask it in writing. Require a written answer. Document the response.
