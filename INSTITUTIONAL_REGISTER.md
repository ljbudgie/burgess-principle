# Institutional Register — SOVEREIGN/NULL Compliance Record

A factual record of every institution tested under The Burgess Principle framework.

---

## Introduction

This register documents every institution that has been tested under The Burgess Principle — a constitutional compliance standard protected by UK Certification Mark UK00004343685. Each institution was asked a single question: was a human mind with proper authority individually applied to the specific facts of the specific case before enforcement action was taken against the individual? The register records the sector, the question asked, the institution's response (or silence), and the resulting SOVEREIGN or NULL finding.

As of 20 April 2026, all completed assessments except one returned a NULL finding. The sole SOVEREIGN finding (Wave Utilities — at point of resolution) demonstrates that the framework identifies compliance equally. Every institution is scored under Institution Audit Taxonomy v1.0.

---

## Taxonomy Key

Each institution is scored across five dimensions, each 0–4. Total is out of 20.

| Band | Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **NULL** | 0–5 | No meaningful human consideration. Full escalation indicated. |
| **Marginal** | 6–10 | Minimal compliance only. Formal challenge and DSAR pressure warranted. |
| **Partial sovereign** | 11–15 | Some human consideration present. Remedy may be achievable without escalation. |
| **SOVEREIGN** | 16–20 | Full human consideration demonstrated and verifiable. Positive precedent. |

**Dimensions:**
- **D1** — Response to the Burgess Principle binary question (0–4)
- **D2** — Evidence of actual individual human review (0–4)
- **D3** — Timeliness relative to statutory obligation (0–4)
- **D4** — Remedy offered and delivered (0–4)
- **D5** — Recurrence / systemic change (0–4)

## Note on SOVEREIGN Findings

SOVEREIGN findings are documented with equal weight in this register. The framework identifies institutional compliance as well as failure. The Wave Utilities resolution (Entry 7) demonstrates what happens when a human mind is present: Erica individually reviewed both accounts, identified every error the automated system had missed, and resolved all issues in a single exchange. The same framework that identifies NULL processing also recognises when an institution meets the standard. The test is neutral — the outcome depends on the institution.

## Verification Protocol

To move from NULL to SOVEREIGN, the entity must provide:

1. **Written confirmation** that a named human being with appropriate authority individually reviewed the specific facts of the specific case.
2. **Name and role** of the reviewer, provided in writing.
3. **Specific facts cited** — the response must reference the actual facts of the case, not generic process assurances.
4. **Register update** — this register is updated via commit to reflect the revised finding and the date of resolution.

The cryptographic verification pathway (SHA-256 commitment hash + Ed25519 sovereign signature) is available for institutions wishing to provide a formally verifiable record. See `iris/` and `iris-gate/` for implementation.
