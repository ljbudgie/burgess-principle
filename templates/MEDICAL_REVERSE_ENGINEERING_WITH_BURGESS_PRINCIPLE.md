# Medical Intervention Review — Reverse-Engineering Harms
**— The Burgess Principle**

## When to Use This Template

Use this template when you or someone you care for has received a medical intervention — a prescription, a surgical procedure, a device fitting, a clinical pathway — and you believe the decision was made without individual human review of the specific facts of your specific situation.

This template is suitable for:

- Patients who experienced adverse outcomes from a clinical pathway and want to understand who individually authorised each decision
- Detransitioners or their families seeking accountability from gender identity services
- Patients harmed by surgical mesh, opioid prescriptions, or device fitting who want a named clinician to account for the decision
- Anyone who was processed through a clinical pathway and received no individual follow-up after an adverse outcome
- Parents or carers acting on behalf of a patient who cannot advocate for themselves

**This template does not require a lawyer.** It asks a simple, binary question. The institution's answer — or their silence — is itself evidence.

**If you have a disability or communication need:** Add the reasonable adjustment line at the top of your letter. Email-only communication is a reasonable adjustment under the Equality Act 2010 ss.20 and 29. You are entitled to it.

## Guidance Before You Send

**Step 1 — Identify each decision node.**
Before filling in the template, write down the key decisions that were made in your clinical pathway. Use the node structure from Paper XI as a guide:

- Who referred you, and on what basis?
- Who made the initial prescription or intervention decision?
- Who decided to progress you from one intervention to the next?
- Who was responsible for your follow-up?
- Which guideline or protocol governed the decisions made about you?

You do not need to have answers to these questions. You are asking the institution to provide them.

**Step 2 — Identify the institution.**
Send this letter to the institution that made the decision — the NHS Trust, the clinic, the GP surgery, the device manufacturer, the prescribing service. Copy the relevant regulatory body if you have already exhausted the institution's complaints process.

**Step 3 — Keep every response.**
Every reply — including automated acknowledgements, weasel-worded non-answers, and silences — is part of your documented record. If the institution responds with vague process language ("subject to clinical oversight," "reviewed in line with protocol"), use the follow-up template at the end of this document.

**Step 4 — Note the statutory timeframes.**
Under UK GDPR Article 15, a Subject Access Request must be responded to within one calendar month. Under the NHS complaints process, an initial response is due within three working days and a full response within 40 working days. Under the Medical Devices Regulations, adverse event reports must be acknowledged. These are not targets. They are legal obligations.

## The Template

---

**Date:** [Insert date]
**Your reference:** [Insert any case, patient, or reference number if known]
**To:** [Name of institution / Data Protection Officer / Clinical Lead / Complaints Team]
**By email only** *(if this is your reasonable adjustment, add: "Email is my sole contact method as a reasonable adjustment under the Equality Act 2010 for [your condition]. I ask that all responses are made by email and that this adjustment is noted on any file opened in connection with this correspondence.")*

---

Dear [Institution / Complaints Team / Data Protection Officer],

I am writing to ask a specific question about a medical decision made about me [or: about [name], for whom I am writing as [relationship / carer / parent]].

The decision I am asking about is:

**[Describe the specific intervention, prescription, procedure, or pathway decision — e.g. "the decision to prescribe puberty blockers at [clinic name] on or around [date]" / "the decision to implant surgical mesh at [hospital] in [year]" / "the decision to continue my opioid prescription at [surgery name] without individual review in [period]"].**

I am applying the Burgess Principle binary test to this decision.

The Burgess Principle (UK Certification Mark UK00004343685) asks one question of any institution that makes a decision affecting an individual:

**"Was a named human being able to personally review the specific facts of my specific situation before this decision was made?"**

The answer is either yes or no. There is no middle ground.

I am asking you to confirm, in writing:

1. **YES or NO** — was a named human being personally present at the decision described above, having individually reviewed the specific facts of my situation before that decision was made?

2. If **YES** — please provide the name and clinical role of the individual who personally reviewed my case, and describe what specific facts of my situation they individually considered.

3. If **NO** — please confirm that no individual human review of my specific facts took place before the decision was made, and explain what process was followed instead.

I am also making a formal **Subject Access Request** under Article 15 of the UK GDPR for all personal data held about me in connection with this decision, including:

- My full clinical record relating to [the intervention / pathway / prescription]
- Any assessment records, referral notes, and decision rationale
- Any records of follow-up, adverse event reporting, or outcome tracking
- The specific clinical guideline or protocol under which the decision was made
- Any record of who individually reviewed my case and when

For clarity: a general statement that your service includes human oversight, or that decisions are made in line with clinical guidelines, does not answer the Burgess Principle question. I am asking whether a named human being personally reviewed the specific facts of my specific situation. Please answer with a direct **YES** or **NO**, and if **YES**, with the name and role of that individual.

I am also requesting, under **UK GDPR Article 22**, confirmation of whether any automated decision-making or profiling contributed to the decision described above, and if so, the right to request human intervention.

I would be grateful for a response within **one calendar month** in accordance with my statutory rights under the UK GDPR.

Thank you for treating this as the individual matter it is.

Yours sincerely,

[Your Full Name]
[Your Email Address]
[Your NHS number or date of birth — optional, may help locate your records]

## Follow-Up: If You Receive a Weasel Response

If the institution responds with language like:

- *"All decisions are subject to clinical oversight"*
- *"Your case was reviewed in line with our protocol"*
- *"Human review is embedded in our pathway"*
- *"We are unable to identify a specific individual reviewer"*

— send this follow-up:

---

Dear [Institution],

Thank you for your response dated [date].

I note that your response does not directly answer the Burgess Principle question I asked.

The question was: **was a named human being able to personally review the specific facts of my specific situation before the decision was made?**

Your response describes a general process or policy. It does not confirm that a named individual personally reviewed my specific facts.

Please answer the question directly:

**YES or NO** — did a named human being personally review the specific facts of my case before the decision described in my letter was made?

If **YES** — please provide their name and role.

If **NO** — please confirm that no individual named review of my specific facts took place.

A response that does not directly answer this question will be recorded as an AMBIGUOUS finding under the Burgess Principle and may be submitted to the relevant regulatory body as evidence of failure to confirm individual human review.

I look forward to your direct response.

Yours sincerely,

[Your Full Name]
[Your Email Address]

## Escalation: Where to Go If the Institution Does Not Respond

| Situation | Escalate to |
|-----------|-------------|
| NHS Trust or GP surgery | NHS England Patient Experience Team; Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman (PHSO) |
| Gender identity service | Care Quality Commission (CQC); NHS England |
| Medical device | Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) |
| Data protection failure | Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) |
| Equality Act breach | Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) |
| Regulatory body failure | Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman; judicial review |

For each escalation, attach:
- Your original letter
- The institution's response (or evidence of non-response)
- A brief statement of the Burgess Principle binary question and the finding: SOVEREIGN, NULL, or AMBIGUOUS

## Recording Your Finding

When you have a response — or a documented non-response — record it using this format:

**Institution:** [Name]
**Decision audited:** [Description of the specific clinical decision]
**Date of audit letter:** [Date]
**Response received:** YES / NO / No response by [deadline date]
**Binary finding:** SOVEREIGN / NULL / AMBIGUOUS
**Evidence:** [Brief description — e.g. "Named reviewer provided: Dr [Name], Clinical Lead" / "Response confirmed no individual review took place" / "No substantive response received within statutory timeframe"]
**Next step:** [Resolved / ICO complaint / PHSO referral / Regulatory escalation]

This record belongs to you. Keep it. It is your evidence trail.

## A Note on Using This Template

This template was built from the methodology in Paper XI of the Burgess Principle — *Reverse-Engineering Harms: A Methodology for Tracing Iatrogenic Injury Through Institutional Failure* — completed on Saturday, 25 April 2026.

The paper demonstrates that in every domain of contested medicine — paediatric gender medicine, opioid prescribing, thalidomide, surgical mesh — the harm that was produced by identifiable institutional decisions shares the same structure: a named human being was absent from a decision that required individual human review of specific facts.

This template gives you the tool to ask, in writing, whether that named human being was present at the decision that affected you.

Their answer — or their silence — is the finding.
