# Case Study: Wave Utilities — Automated Debt Enforcement Against a Disabled Customer

**Case Reference:** Wave / iC&R — Accounts 31694322001 & 31694322002  
**Sector:** Water Retail (Non-Household)  
**Current Status:** RESOLVED  
**Date Opened:** 09 December 2025  
**Date Resolved:** 30 March 2026  
**Current Finding:** MIXED — NULL at referral, SOVEREIGN at resolution  
**Response Classification:** AMBIGUOUS  
**Procedural Posture:** Closed after Complex Queries review corrected both accounts and withdrew enforcement.  
**Last Updated:** 12 April 2026  
**Outcome:** Both accounts cleared to £0.00. £795.14 in fees and interest removed. All enforcement ceased and reasonable adjustments were recorded.

---

## Summary

Wave Utilities referred two water accounts to debt collection agent iC&R through an automated threshold process before any individual reviewed the specific facts. The system pursued £679.38 from a disabled, self-employed customer whose actual combined balance was approximately £35 after payments already in the system. When Erica in Wave's Complex Queries team finally reviewed the accounts, every issue resolved in a single exchange. This remains the clearest completed example in the repository of a NULL process becoming SOVEREIGN once a real person looked.

## Background

- **Complainant / applicant:** Self-employed business operator at a redacted premises.
- **Relevant account, document, or reference:** Accounts 31694322001 and 31694322002.
- **Disability / adjustment context:** Bilateral sensorineural hearing loss; email-only communication asserted under section 20 of the Equality Act 2010.
- **What the institution did:** Wave automatically referred both accounts to iC&R, which attended the business premises and threatened disconnection while late-payment charges continued to accumulate.
- **Why the case matters:** It shows the entire Burgess Principle cycle in one place: automated enforcement, a written question, a named reviewer, and full correction.

---

## The SOVEREIGN / NULL Question

> "Confirmation of whether any individual at iC&R verified the current account balance before attending my premises or threatening disconnection."

This question isolates the key enforcement step: whether anyone checked the live balance, disability context, and proportionality before threatening a disabled customer with water disconnection.

---

## Institutional Response and Classification

### Response relied on

> "This is an automatic process in the system where accounts get referrals after they hit a certain amount of outstanding debt. However, before referring the accounts to the accredited entities (ICR) they go through a series of checks. The decisions are based on the Wave T&C's, Wave policies and procedures and all applicable laws/regulations related to the water industry, debt collection and Accredited Entities."

### Why this classification applies

- **Current finding: MIXED.** The operative referral was NULL, but the final correction was SOVEREIGN because Erica individually reviewed the live facts and resolved the matter.
- **Response classification: AMBIGUOUS.** Wave admitted that referrals were triggered automatically, but wrapped that admission in generic language about "a series of checks" without naming any reviewer who checked this specific case before iC&R acted.
- **What the evidence still proves:** No individual reviewed the balance, payments, disability status, or proportionality before enforcement began.

### Next follow-up needed

- Preserve the correspondence and outcome summary as a model case.
- Reuse the same question and the same written-evidence approach in future debt or enforcement disputes.
