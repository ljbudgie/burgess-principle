# Follow-Up After a Vague or Evasive Response  
**— The Burgess Principle**

**Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Reference:** [Your Case / Account / Decision Reference]  
**To:** [Institution / Team]

Dear [Institution / Team],

Thank you for your recent reply.

Your response appears to describe your general process, for example by referring to "human oversight", review "in line with policy", or decisions that are "subject to human review". I am grateful for the reply, but that kind of general wording does not answer the specific Burgess Principle question for my case.

For clarity, I am asking for a direct answer to this question:

**Was a human member of your team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific situation before the decision or action in question was taken?**

A general statement about your wider systems or policies does not confirm that a human member of your team personally reviewed the specific facts of my case.

Please therefore reply with a clear **YES** or **NO**.

- If **YES**, please provide the **name and role** of the person who carried out that review, or a specific internal reference that identifies them.
- If **NO**, please confirm that the matter was handled without individual human scrutiny of the specific facts, and let me know what steps you will now take to provide that review.

If it helps, I am not asking for a description of your usual policy. I am asking about the specific facts of my own case.

Thank you for your time. I would be grateful for a direct written response within [7 / 14] days.

Yours sincerely,  
[Your Full Name]  
[Your Contact Details]
