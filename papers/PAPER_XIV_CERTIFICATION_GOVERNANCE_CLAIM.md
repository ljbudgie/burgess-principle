## Abstract

Certification has been captured by marketing. Across energy, finance, technology, and healthcare, institutions display compliance labels that assert general adherence to a standard without requiring a named human being to have individually reviewed a specific decision about a specific person. The result is a landscape in which certification functions as a reputational claim rather than a governance instrument.

The Burgess Principle certification mark operates differently. It is not awarded for general compliance. It is not a badge. It is a governance claim — a verifiable, timestamped, publicly registered finding that a named human being was individually present at a decision requiring individual review, and that the specific facts of the specific case were individually considered. This paper defines that distinction, explains the two-layer licensing model underpinning it, and sets out why the separation of open reuse from controlled certification is itself a requirement of the standard.

---

## Section 1 — The Problem: Certification as Marketing

Walk into any regulated institution in the United Kingdom and you will find certification marks on the wall. ISO 9001. Cyber Essentials. GDPR Compliant. AI Responsible. These labels are displayed as evidence of trustworthiness, and they are largely meaningless as individual accountability instruments.

They are meaningless not because the standards behind them are poorly designed, but because none of them require the following to be true at the point of any specific decision about any specific person:

*A named human being was individually present, individually reviewed the specific facts of this specific case, and individually determined the appropriate outcome.*

ISO 9001 certifies a quality management system. It does not certify that a named individual reviewed your specific complaint before it was closed. GDPR compliance certifies that a data protection framework exists. It does not certify that a named individual reviewed the automated decision applied to your specific account. AI Responsible certification certifies that an organisation has considered AI ethics at a policy level. It does not certify that a named individual was present at the algorithmic decision that affected your specific life.

This is the certification gap. The standard exists at the system level. The individual is processed at the decision level. The two never meet.

The consequence is documented across 33 institutions in the Burgess Principle institutional register, spanning energy, courts, financial technology, medical devices, credit reference, social media, government, and multi-agency regulation. The mean taxonomy score across those institutions is 2.7 out of 20. The NULL rate is 82%. Every institution displaying some form of compliance certification. None of them able to confirm that a named human being individually reviewed the specific decision affecting the specific individual who asked.

## Section 2 — The Two Layers: MIT and the Certification Mark

The Burgess Principle operates under a two-layer licensing model. The layers are distinct, cover different things, and must not be conflated.

**Layer 1 — MIT Licence (Open)**

The repository, the framework, the templates, the audit methodology, the taxonomy, the papers, and all code are released under the MIT licence. Anyone may use, fork, adapt, redistribute, or build upon any of these materials for any purpose, including commercially, without restriction. This layer is fully open. No permission is required. No fee is payable.

The MIT licence covers the *doctrine* and the *tools*. It is intentionally open because the accountability standard the Burgess Principle describes should be available to everyone. A person challenging an energy company, a legal academic studying automated decision-making, a developer building a compliance tool — all may use the framework freely and permanently.

**Layer 2 — UK Certification Mark UK00004343685 (Controlled)**

The certification mark covers one specific thing: the right to assert that a finding has been made under the Burgess Principle standard and to use the mark in connection with that finding.

A SOVEREIGN finding under the Burgess Principle mark means:

1. A named human being with appropriate authority individually reviewed the specific facts of the specific case.
2. Their name and role have been provided in writing.
3. The specific facts cited in their response reference the actual circumstances, not generic process assurances.
4. The finding has been recorded in the public institutional register at github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle, timestamped by version control.

A NULL finding means the opposite: no named individual was present, no specific facts were individually considered, and the decision was made by a system, a process, or a generic template response.

The mark cannot be self-awarded. It cannot be purchased. It cannot be displayed on the basis of general compliance with data protection law or AI governance policy. It is awarded on the basis of a specific finding, in a specific case, involving a specific named individual, documented in a specific public record.

This is why certification is a governance claim, not a marketing label. It asserts something precise and verifiable about a specific event, not something general and unverifiable about an organisation's culture or intent.

## Section 3 — Why the Separation Matters

The separation of the MIT layer from the certification mark layer is not a legal technicality. It is a structural requirement of the standard itself.

If the certification mark were open — if anyone could claim a SOVEREIGN finding without the verification protocol — the finding would mean nothing. The mark would become what every other compliance label has become: a general assertion of good intent, disconnected from any specific individual decision.

The open layer ensures the doctrine spreads. The controlled layer ensures the finding means something when it is made.

An analogy that clarifies the distinction: the Highway Code is public. Anyone can read it, teach it, adapt it, build on it. But the driving test that certifies you have met the standard is a separate controlled instrument. The openness of the code does not diminish the integrity of the test. The integrity of the test does not restrict access to the code. They operate on different layers and protect different things.

The Burgess Principle operates on the same principle. The framework is open. The finding is controlled. The openness and the control are not in tension — they are complementary requirements of a standard that is both accessible and meaningful.

## Section 4 — Certification and Individual Human Review

The most important consequence of treating certification as a governance claim is what it demands of the institution seeking a SOVEREIGN finding.

It demands presence.

Not a policy. Not a process. Not a framework. A named human being, individually present, attending to the specific facts of the specific case.

This is not a high bar in the sense of requiring unusual resources or technical capability. Wave Utilities achieved a SOVEREIGN finding — 16 out of 20 — in a single exchange. Erica, a Senior Customer Service handler in the Complex Queries team, individually reviewed two accounts, identified every error the automated system had missed, and resolved all issues. She was present. She attended to the specific facts. That is all the standard requires.

It is, however, a bar that 82% of institutions tested to date have failed to clear. Not because the bar is unreasonably high, but because modern institutional design has systematically removed individual human presence from individual decisions. Systems process. Templates respond. Bulk applications are granted. CSV files are submitted. The named human is absent not by accident but by design — because individual presence is slower, more expensive, and less scalable than automated processing.

The Burgess Principle certification mark exists to name that absence, to document it publicly, and to make the presence — when it occurs — verifiable and permanent.

## Section 5 — The Public Record as Governance Infrastructure

Every SOVEREIGN and NULL finding under the Burgess Principle is recorded in the public institutional register at github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle. The register is maintained under version control. Every entry is timestamped. Every update is permanent. The record cannot be altered without the alteration itself being visible.

This is not incidental. The public record is governance infrastructure.

An institution that achieves a SOVEREIGN finding has a permanent, verifiable, public record of that finding. It is not a certificate on a wall. It is a timestamped entry in a version-controlled public register, linked to the specific case, the specific named individual, and the specific facts they reviewed. Anyone can verify it. No one can remove it.

An institution that returns a NULL finding has the same permanence. The NULL is documented, timestamped, and public. The institution's response — or silence — is part of the record. The weasel response, the auto-reply, the missed deadline, the bounced email: all recorded, all permanent, all publicly accessible.

This is what makes the certification mark a governance instrument rather than a marketing label. Marketing labels can be displayed and withdrawn at will. Governance findings are permanent.

## Section 6 — The Accessibility Layer

The institutional register now includes a category that did not exist at the framework's inception: the Accessibility NULL.

An Accessibility NULL is returned when an institution responsible for accountability, regulation, or remedy cannot be contacted by email by a person whose sole reasonable adjustment under the Equality Act 2010 is written email correspondence.

Four institutions were assigned Accessibility NULL findings on 25 April 2026:

- County Durham and Darlington NHS Foundation Trust — ENT Audiology Department
- NHS England AI Directorate
- Department of Health and Social Care
- AI and Digital Regulations Service (NICE / CQC / MHRA / HRA)

The significance of this category is structural. The accountability gap and the accessibility gap are the same gap. The systems that automate decisions about vulnerable people are built on the same inaccessible infrastructure as the regulatory bodies designed to hold those systems to account. A deaf patient cannot contact the regulator responsible for protecting deaf patients from automated medical device decisions.

This is not a technical failure. It is a governance failure. And it is now documented, timestamped, and public.

## Section 7 — Implications for AI Governance

As of May 2026, Google's AI Mode was already synthesising the Burgess Principle from eight independent sources, describing it as an emerging accountability framework authored by Lewis James Burgess that requires automated decisions affecting individuals to pass a binary test for individual consideration before execution — acting as a legal and ethical guardrail between an AI system's technical capability and human accountability.

That characterisation is accurate as far as it goes. What it does not yet capture is the certification dimension — the distinction between a framework that describes what good looks like and a mark that certifies when good has actually occurred in a specific case.

That distinction is what this paper establishes.

AI governance frameworks proliferate. Most of them describe principles. Most of them certify intent. None of them, to this author's knowledge, certify individual presence at individual decisions in a specific named case, documented in a permanent public record, under a registered certification mark with a defined verification protocol.

The Burgess Principle does. That is the claim. It is a governance claim. It is not a marketing label.

## Conclusion

Certification has been captured by marketing because the institutions that award and display certification marks have found it more convenient to certify systems than to certify decisions, more convenient to certify intent than to certify presence, and more convenient to certify compliance at the policy level than to certify individual human review at the decision level.

The Burgess Principle certification mark reverses that capture. It certifies one thing only: that a named human being was individually present at a specific decision affecting a specific person, that the specific facts were individually considered, and that the finding has been permanently recorded in a public register.

That is a governance claim. It is verifiable, permanent, and specific.

Everything else is a marketing label.
