## Doctrinal Notes

The passport case matters because it asks whether a compulsory identity instrument can be issued without any named person appearing at the point of decision. That makes it a strong test case for the repository's newer distinction between visible institutional authority and actual human accountability.

## Appendix A — Core Article 22 and Equality Act Analysis

Biometric passport processing appears to involve automated capture, matching, and database checks that produce legal effects for the applicant. The operative Article 22 question is whether meaningful human intervention occurred in this specific application before the passport was issued. The Equality Act question runs in parallel: if the applicant required email-only communication or another adjustment, where in the issuance flow could that individual circumstance actually be seen and acted on by a named person?
