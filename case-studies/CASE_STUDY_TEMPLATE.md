## Replicability Guidance

### The Single Key SOVEREIGN Test Question

Any citizen facing automated enforcement should ask one question:

> **"Was a human being with proper authority individually applied to the specific facts of my specific case before this action was taken against me?"**

Ask it in writing. Require a written answer. Document the response.

### Practical Escalation Steps That Worked

1. **Identify the automated trigger.** Request confirmation of whether the enforcement referral was an automatic process or involved individual human review.
2. **State the question in writing.** Send a single, clear, written challenge to the institution (email preferred for audit trail). Quote the institution's own data where possible — attach their invoices, payment records, or statements that contradict the enforcement.
3. **Assert your reasonable adjustment.** If you have a disability, notify the institution in writing and request a specific adjustment (e.g. email-only communication under Equality Act 2010 s.20). Do not provide medical evidence unless legally required — the burden is on the institution to make adjustments, not on you to prove you need them.
4. **Escalate to a named human decision-maker.** Request that your complaint be handled by a named individual in a complex queries, complaints, or senior review team. Automated complaint handlers inherit the same NULL defect.
5. **Document every response.** Save every email, letter, and reference number. Note dates of premises attendances. Record names of any enforcement agents who attend.
6. **Use institutional silence as evidence.** If the institution does not answer the SOVEREIGN question within a reasonable period (14–28 days), their silence is itself evidence that no individual review occurred. Log this in your timeline.
7. **Cross-reference the institution's own records.** The strongest evidence is always the institution's own data contradicting its enforcement. If their invoices show you owe £35 but their agent is pursuing £679, attach both documents side by side.
8. **Maintain calm, factual tone throughout.** The principle does not require confrontation, legal training, or representation. It requires one question, asked clearly, answered in writing, and documented.
