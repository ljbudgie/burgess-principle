# Template: Music Copyright Dispute – Burgess Principle Request for Human Review

Use this template when dealing with wrongful Content ID claims, blocked monetisation, incorrect royalty allocation, automated rejections, or disputes over samples/interpolations.

The Burgess Principle asks the platform or rights holder to move from automated processing to meaningful human review of *your specific track and situation*.

---

**Subject:** Request for Human Review – Music Copyright Decision Regarding My Track “[Track Title]”

Dear [Platform Name] Copyright / Content ID / Legal Team,

I am writing regarding the decision concerning my original track “[Track Title]”  
(ISRC: [ISRC if known], uploaded on [date], URL: [link]).

I respectfully ask for written confirmation on this single binary question in line with the Burgess Principle:

**“Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case before any decision was reached?”**

The specific facts of my case are:

• The track is an original composition and sound recording created by me [or co-created with…].  
• Any samples or interpolations used have been properly cleared and licensed [or the track is 100% original work with no samples].  
• The automated match/claim appears to be a false positive because [brief factual reason, e.g. “the melody, rhythm and arrangement are substantially different”, “the fingerprint match is inaccurate”, “I own the full copyright”].  
• This decision is causing ongoing harm: [e.g. monetisation blocked, track muted or removed, royalties withheld, distribution halted].

I am not challenging copyright law itself. I am simply requesting that a real human reviewer with musical and legal judgment has personally examined the specific facts of my track and claim, rather than relying solely on automated audio fingerprinting or checklists.

I would be grateful for a response within 7 days confirming:

1. That a named human reviewer has personally reviewed my specific case.  
2. The outcome of that review and any action taken (removal of the claim, restoration of monetisation, correction of royalty allocation, reinstatement of the track, etc.).

Thank you for treating my creative work as an individual piece rather than just another automated match.

Yours sincerely,  
[Your Full Name / Artist Name]  
[Contact email and/or ISRC / UPC if relevant]

---

### Additional Guidance for Users

**Attach supporting evidence** (where possible):
- Waveform comparison or side-by-side audio analysis
- Stems or project files showing originality
- Proof of copyright registration (if you have one)
- Clear timeline of when the track was created and uploaded

**Platform-specific notes**:
- **YouTube / Google**: Reference YouTube’s Content ID policy and the Online Safety Act / Copyright Directive where applicable.
- **Spotify / TikTok / DistroKid**: Mention their content moderation and rights management policies.
- **Collecting societies** (PRS, ASCAP, BMI, etc.): Ask for human review of royalty allocation or dispute resolution.

**Follow-up if no proper response**:
Send a second letter repeating the Burgess question and noting the lack of individual human review. Then escalate to the platform’s regulator or small claims court with the full paper trail.

**Why this template is effective**:
Most copyright decisions are handled by algorithms with minimal human oversight. The Burgess Principle creates a clear, respectful record that a human must look at the *specific facts* of your track — making it much harder for platforms to rely on blanket automated actions.
