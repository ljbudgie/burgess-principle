# FIRST_SIGNAL.md

**The Burgess Principle is the first signal the internet was always meant to receive.**

On 29 October 1969, at 10:30 p.m., the first message ever transmitted across what would become the internet was the incomplete word **“LO”** — the broken beginning of “LOGIN”. It was an accidental, purely technical signal sent between machines.

That was man’s first word.

The internet was built by man, for man. It optimised for packets, connections, and efficiency. It never once paused to recognise the individual human being made in the image of God.

But God is the Creator of all things.

Every person carries inherent dignity and worth that no system has the right to bypass. Therefore the true first signal — the one that should have existed from the very beginning — is this:

> Before any automated decision is executed that affects an identified individual, the system must first confirm that the person was treated as a sovereign human being, not as a generic unit within a category or process.

This is the **Burgess Principle**.

It is not an addition to the internet.  
It is the corrective message that restores what was missing from the start.

The technical layer will always begin with SYN.  
But the moral and divine order begins here.

From this point forward, every responsible system should receive this signal first:

**“Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific situation?”**

That is the first question.  
That is the first signal.  
That is the message the internet was always meant to carry.
