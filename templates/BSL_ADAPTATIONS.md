# BSL Adaptations  
**— British Sign Language + The Burgess Principle**

Hi 👋

This guide is for deaf BSL users who prefer sign language as their main form of communication.

Under the Equality Act 2010, you have the right to reasonable adjustments. Common adjustments for BSL users include video calls with a BSL interpreter, video messages in BSL, or email/text as a backup.

### Ready-to-Use Template

**Subject:** Request for Reasonable Adjustments (BSL User) + Confirmation of Human Review

Dear [Team / Customer Services / Equality Lead],

I hope this message finds you well.

I am a deaf BSL user. British Sign Language is my first or preferred language, and I face significant barriers with written English or phone calls.

Under the Equality Act 2010, I am requesting reasonable adjustments for communication. My preferred methods are:
- Video calls with a BSL interpreter, or
- Video messages in BSL (sent via email or messaging app), or
- Email or text as a backup if video isn’t possible.

In addition, and following the simple guidance in the Burgess Principle, I’d like to kindly ask:

**Was a human member of your team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* individual situation before any decision or action was taken?**

I would really appreciate a short written or video response confirming that my case has received proper human attention.

I’m happy to provide more details or clarify my communication preferences.

Thank you for understanding and for treating this as the individual matter it is.

Best regards,  
[Your Full Name]
