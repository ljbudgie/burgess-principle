## Guidance Note for Urgent Medical Review Template

**When to use this template**  
Use this when you or a loved one is seriously ill, in significant pain, or showing signs of deterioration, and the healthcare system is not providing timely individual attention (delayed appointments, automated triage, or generic responses).

**Tips for best results**
- Keep the symptom description factual and brief — focus on what is happening and how it is affecting daily life.
- Attach any recent test results, GP letters, or symptom notes if possible. This helps the clinician see the urgency faster.
- Send the letter to both the GP surgery and the relevant hospital department/PALS (Patient Advice and Liaison Service) if the person is under a trust.
- If you receive a generic or automated reply, send a short follow-up letter repeating the key question and noting the lack of individual clinical review.
- Save all correspondence — it creates a strong paper trail if you need to escalate to the formal NHS complaints process or Ombudsman.

This template is designed to be respectful while creating accountability. It asks the system to do what it should already be doing: have a qualified clinician look at the specific facts of the individual case.

Remember: the goal is to get the person seen and helped as quickly as possible.

# Template: Urgent Care Review – Request for Timely Human Attention

Use this when you or a loved one is seriously ill, in significant pain, or deteriorating, and the healthcare system is not providing timely individual attention (delayed appointments, automated triage, or generic responses).

---

**Subject:** Urgent Request for Clinical Review – My [or My Loved One's] Serious Medical Situation

Dear [GP Surgery / Hospital Department / NHS Trust] Team,

I am writing because I [or my loved one] is currently experiencing a serious medical situation that requires urgent attention.

I respectfully ask for written confirmation on this single binary question:

**"Was a qualified clinician able to personally review the specific facts of this specific case before any decision on prioritisation or next steps was reached?"**

The specific facts of this case are:

• I [or my loved one] have been experiencing [brief, factual description of symptoms and how long they have been going on].
• This is causing significant pain, distress, and risk of further deterioration.
• Previous requests for [appointment / investigation / treatment / review] have not resulted in timely individual attention.

I am not asking for special treatment beyond what is medically appropriate. I am simply requesting that a qualified clinician with medical judgment has personally reviewed the specific facts of this case.

Given the seriousness of the situation, I would be grateful for a response within **48–72 hours** confirming:

1. That a named qualified clinician has personally reviewed this specific case.  
2. The outcome of that clinical review and the immediate next steps (e.g. urgent appointment, further investigations, or specialist referral).

If a timely individual clinical review is not possible, please explain why and provide a clear escalation pathway.

Thank you for treating me [or my loved one] as a real person who deserves to be seen and helped when unwell.

Yours sincerely,  
[Your Full Name]  
[Relationship to the patient if advocating for someone else, e.g. Son / Daughter / Carer]  
[Contact phone and email]  
[NHS number or date of birth if helpful]
