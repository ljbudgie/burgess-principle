The Burgess Principle is open so ordinary people can use it without friction. The
repository materials are MIT licensed. The official certification mark is governed
separately so nobody can sell or badge an unchanged automated process as
"Burgess compliant" without real individual human review.

This document separates those two things.

## 4. The Governance Boundary

The certification mark (**UK00004343685**) exists to protect the meaning of the
standard. It is not a paywall around the repository. It is a public trust signal
that the certified process has been checked against the core governance question:

> Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of
> my specific situation?

A process may not be presented as **Burgess Certified**, **official**,
**approved**, or **certified compliant** unless the proprietor has authorised that
use of the mark.

This boundary protects individuals from institutional badging. A company cannot
keep making automated decisions about people, add the words "Burgess Principle",
and call the result compliant.

Permission is required before you use the Burgess Principle name, certification
mark, official branding, or equivalent trust signal to suggest approval,

Examples include:

- marketing a product, service, workflow, legal pack, audit, or AI system as
  **Burgess Certified** or officially compliant;

- placing the mark on institutional policies, procurement materials, dashboards,

Independent commercial use of the MIT-licensed materials is allowed. Official
certified use of the mark is separate.

## 6. Certification Standard

Certification is available only where the process preserves meaningful human
involvement for decisions affecting identified individuals. A certified process
must, at minimum:

1. apply the binary test before action is taken;
2. identify a named human reviewer with authority to stop, change, or approve the
   action;
3. require that reviewer to examine the specific facts of the specific person's
   situation;
4. record the SOVEREIGN / NULL / AMBIGUOUS outcome and the basis for it;
5. escalate NULL or AMBIGUOUS outcomes for individual human review rather than
   treating them as approval;
6. preserve accessibility duties, safety duties, legal obligations, and platform
   security rules; and
7. maintain records sufficient for review, renewal, suspension, or revocation.

Certification does not mean the repository replaces the law. It means the
certified process has committed to the human-scrutiny standard the repository
exists to make visible.

## 7. Certification Routes

The governance route depends on who is being certified, not on who can pay the
most.

| Route | Typical applicant | Governance focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Open / individual use** | Individuals, researchers, open-source maintainers | MIT use with attribution; no mark claim |
| **Practitioner certification** | Sole traders, advisers, community practitioners | Each client matter personally reviewed by a named human |
| **Institutional certification** | Public bodies, companies, platforms, charities | Decision processes affecting identified people include real human scrutiny |
| **Technology certification** | AI agents, workflow tools, developer platforms | Burgess gate is built before decision logic and cannot be bypassed by prompts |
| **Litigation / advocacy partners** | Law firms, funders, claims groups, advocates | Use of the framework in client matters with individual review and clear records |

Commercial terms, if any, are handled in a separate written agreement. Public
interest, accessibility, and community uses may be licensed at nominal or no cost
where that best serves the governance purpose.

## 8. Public Record, Renewal, and Revocation

Certified uses should be traceable. Where appropriate, certification may be
recorded in this repository or another public register with:

- the certified person, organisation, process, or tool;
- the scope of certification;
- the issue and renewal dates;
- any conditions or limitations; and
- the contact route for verification.

Certification may be refused, suspended, or revoked if a certified party:

- relies on vague process language instead of individual review;
- uses the mark outside the authorised scope;
- claims certification for automated decisions that still return NULL or
  AMBIGUOUS without escalation;
- misleads affected people about what was reviewed; or
- undermines accessibility, legal, safety, or security obligations.

---

## 9. Anti-Monetisation Guardrail

The project does not support selling "Burgess Principle compliant" products that
continue to rely on automated decisions about individuals without meaningful
human involvement. Certification is not a badge for automation. It is evidence
that a real human mind can be applied to the specific facts before power is
exercised.
