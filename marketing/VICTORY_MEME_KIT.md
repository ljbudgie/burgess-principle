# Helpful Memes & Sharing Ideas

Hi 👋

Here are some gentle, positive ways to share the Burgess Principle with others. The goal is simple: help more people feel seen and treated as real individuals rather than numbers in a system.

### Suggested Short Posts (for X, Threads, LinkedIn, etc.)

**Post 1**  
"Have you ever felt like a big system just didn’t see you as a real person?  

The Burgess Principle is a simple, respectful way to ask any institution:  

‘Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of *my* situation?’  

It’s free, easy to use, and works with your existing rights.  

The full toolkit is here → github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle  
Drop it into any AI and get personalised help in seconds."

**Post 2**  
"Sometimes one calm question can make a real difference.  

The Burgess Principle helps you politely ask for human attention on your specific case — whether it’s a bill, a warrant, a credit decision, or something else.  

It’s completely free for personal use.  
Link in bio if you’d like to try it."

**Post 3** (especially good for neurodiversity communities)  
"If you’re autistic or need reasonable adjustments (like email-only contact), the Burgess Principle pairs nicely with your rights under the Equality Act.  

It’s a respectful way to ask that a real person looks at your individual situation.  

Free templates and AI help available here → github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle"

### Gentle Meme Ideas (text versions – easy to turn into images)

1. **Simple & Warm**  
   Image: A friendly person waving at a computer screen  
   Caption: “Could a real human please look at my specific situation?  
   The Burgess Principle – because everyone deserves to be seen.”

2. **Two-Panel**  
   Left panel: Robot stamping papers quickly  
   Right panel: Kind human carefully reading one file  
   Caption: “Bulk processed vs actually seen.  
   One simple question changes everything — Burgess Principle”

3. **Hopeful**  
   Image: Someone looking calm while typing on their phone  
   Caption: “Instead of feeling invisible to the system, you can politely ask for human attention.  
   The Burgess Principle is free to try.”

### Sharing Tips
- Keep the tone kind and hopeful — people respond better to warmth than urgency.
- Always include the GitHub link so others can easily find the free toolkit.
- Use #BurgessPrinciple so people can discover the original repo.

Feel free to adapt any of these ideas. The most important thing is helping more ordinary people feel seen and respected.
