# Ombudsman / Regulator Complaint

A template for escalating to the relevant ombudsman or regulator when an institution fails to respond to your requests, fails to answer the specific questions asked, or fails to meet its legal obligations.

Based on the actual ombudsman referrals filed and prepared in the E.ON, British Gas, Equifax, Darlington Borough Council, and Vanquis cases.

---

## Which Regulator?

Before using this template, identify the correct body for your complaint:

| Sector | Regulator / Ombudsman |
|--------|----------------------|
| Data protection (DSAR non-compliance, Article 22) | [Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)](https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/) |
| Energy (gas and electricity suppliers) | [Energy Ombudsman](https://www.energyombudsman.org/) |
| Financial services (banks, credit cards, loans) | [Financial Ombudsman Service](https://www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk/) |
| Local government (councils, council tax, parking) | [Local Government and Social Care Ombudsman](https://www.lgo.org.uk/) |
| Water (water companies) | [Consumer Council for Water](https://www.ccwater.org.uk/) |
| MP conduct and parliamentary standards | [Parliamentary Commissioner for Standards](https://www.parliament.uk/mps-lords-and-offices/standards-and-financial-interests/parliamentary-commissioner-for-standards/) |
| Credit reference agencies | [Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)](https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/) for data issues; [Financial Ombudsman Service](https://www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk/) for disputes about credit file entries |

## Template

```
Subject: Formal Complaint — [INSTITUTION NAME] — [YOUR REFERENCE NUMBER]

Dear [OMBUDSMAN/REGULATOR NAME],

I am writing to make a formal complaint about [INSTITUTION NAME] in
connection with the following matter.

SUMMARY OF COMPLAINT
[BRIEF SUMMARY OF WHAT HAPPENED — 2-3 SENTENCES]

TIMELINE OF CORRESPONDENCE

[DATE 1]: [WHAT YOU SENT OR WHAT HAPPENED]
[DATE 2]: [WHAT THEY RESPONDED, OR NOTE THAT THEY DID NOT RESPOND]
[DATE 3]: [ANY FOLLOW-UP]
[DATE 4]: [ANY FURTHER CORRESPONDENCE]
(Add as many dates as needed)

WHAT WAS ASKED

On [DATE OF YOUR ORIGINAL REQUEST], I made the following specific
requests of [INSTITUTION NAME]:

1. [FIRST SPECIFIC QUESTION OR REQUEST]
2. [SECOND SPECIFIC QUESTION OR REQUEST]
3. [THIRD SPECIFIC QUESTION OR REQUEST]
(List all specific questions or requests you made)

WHAT WAS — OR WAS NOT — ANSWERED

[INSTITUTION NAME] responded on [DATE OF RESPONSE] / has not responded
as of [TODAY'S DATE].

Their response:
- [DID / DID NOT] address point 1 above. [BRIEF EXPLANATION]
- [DID / DID NOT] address point 2 above. [BRIEF EXPLANATION]
- [DID / DID NOT] address point 3 above. [BRIEF EXPLANATION]

STATUTORY BASIS FOR THIS COMPLAINT

This complaint is based on the following:

[SELECT AND KEEP THE GROUNDS THAT APPLY. DELETE THE OTHERS.]

- Failure to respond to a Subject Access Request within 30 days, in
  breach of Article 12(3) UK GDPR.
- Failure to provide meaningful information about automated
  decision-making, in breach of Article 15(1)(h) UK GDPR.
- Decision made by automated processing without meaningful human
  intervention, in breach of Article 22 UK GDPR.
- Failure to make reasonable adjustments under section 20 of the
  Equality Act 2010.
- Enforcement conduct — [DESCRIBE THE SPECIFIC CONDUCT COMPLAINED OF].
- [ANY OTHER STATUTORY GROUND]

OUTCOME SOUGHT

I am seeking the following outcome:

[SELECT AND KEEP THE OUTCOMES THAT APPLY. DELETE THE OTHERS.]

- A complete and compliant response to my Subject Access Request.
- Confirmation that a human individually reviewed my specific case.
- Compensation for distress and/or inconvenience caused.
- A finding that [INSTITUTION NAME] breached its obligations.
- An order that [INSTITUTION NAME] implement the reasonable adjustments
  I have requested.
- [ANY OTHER SPECIFIC OUTCOME]

SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS

I have attached / can provide the following documents:
- Copy of my original request dated [DATE]
- Copy of [INSTITUTION NAME]'s response dated [DATE] (if any)
- [ANY OTHER SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS]

IDENTITY VERIFICATION
- Full name: [YOUR NAME]
- Address: [YOUR ADDRESS]
- Email: [YOUR EMAIL]
- Reference with [INSTITUTION NAME]: [ACCOUNT/REFERENCE NUMBER]

Yours faithfully,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
```

## Placeholder Fields

| Placeholder | What to enter |
|-------------|---------------|
| `[YOUR NAME]` | Your full legal name |
| `[YOUR ADDRESS]` | Your current postal address |
| `[YOUR EMAIL]` | Your email address |
| `[INSTITUTION NAME]` | The organisation you are complaining about |
| `[ACCOUNT/REFERENCE NUMBER]` | Your account or reference number with that organisation |
| `[YOUR REFERENCE NUMBER]` | Any complaint reference you already have |
| `[OMBUDSMAN/REGULATOR NAME]` | The name of the ombudsman or regulator (see table above) |
| `[DATE 1], [DATE 2]`, etc. | The dates of each step in your correspondence timeline |
| `[BRIEF SUMMARY]` | A 2-3 sentence summary of your complaint |
| `[TODAY'S DATE]` | Today's date |

## Tips for an Effective Complaint

1. **Be factual, not emotional.** State what happened, when, and what was or wasn't answered. The ombudsman will draw their own conclusions.

2. **Be specific about what wasn't answered.** Don't just say "they didn't respond properly." List the exact questions you asked and note which ones were ignored.

3. **Include dates for everything.** A clear timeline is the single most powerful element of a complaint.

4. **Attach your correspondence.** Include copies of your original request and their response (if any). The ombudsman needs to see both sides.

5. **State what outcome you want.** Be clear about what would resolve the complaint for you.

6. **Don't exaggerate.** Understated, factual complaints are taken more seriously than emotional ones.

## After Sending

- Record the date you submitted the complaint.
- The ombudsman or regulator will usually acknowledge receipt and provide a case reference.
- Response times vary — the Energy Ombudsman typically resolves cases within 6-8 weeks; the ICO may take longer.
- If the ombudsman rules in your favour, that ruling can form the foundation for court proceedings if needed. See the [Court Application Guide](TEMPLATE_COURT_APPLICATION.md).
