## The originating case (May 2025)

Enforcement agents arrived at a residential property with no individual human review of the circumstances. That experience of being treated as invisible led to the creation of the Burgess Principle — one calm question that asks institutions to personally review the specific facts of a person's case.

Outcome: it became the starting point for the wider framework and later informed multiple regulatory complaints and follow-up actions.

## Wave Utilities (early 2026)

The principle was applied to two accounts involving billing errors and automated enforcement. After a Burgess-style request for human review, a senior customer service representative personally reviewed the cases.

Outcome: both accounts were resolved to £0.00 after human intervention. For the full documented version, see the Wave case study in [`case-studies/`](../case-studies/CASE_STUDY_WAVE.md).

## Community stories

**Story 1 — Energy bill & CCJ**  
“I received a big energy bill and a CCJ for a period before I even moved in. I was panicking. I used the `GENERAL_DISPUTE_WITH_BURGESS_PRINCIPLE` template and simply asked if a human had looked at my actual move-in date. Scottish Power replied within two weeks with the correct meter readings and withdrew the CCJ. I didn’t have to go to court. For the first time I felt like someone actually listened.”

**Story 2 — Equality Act & email-only request**  
“I’m autistic and phone calls cause me severe anxiety. I sent the Equality Act template to my local council asking for email-only contact plus human review of my housing application. They agreed to email-only within days and a case worker personally reviewed my file. The adjustment made everything less overwhelming. I finally felt seen.”

**Story 3 — Simple DSAR follow-up**  
“I used the `DSAR_WITH_BURGESS_PRINCIPLE` template with my bank after an automated credit decision. I politely asked if a real person had reviewed my specific circumstances. They replied with a named reviewer and corrected an error on my file. It was such a relief to know a human had actually looked.”
