# Template: Music Copyright Dispute – Burgess Principle Request for Human Review

Use this when facing wrongful Content ID claims, blocked monetisation, incorrect royalty allocation, automated rejections, or disputes involving samples and interpolations.

The Burgess Principle asks the platform or rights holder to move from purely automated processing to meaningful human review of *your specific track and situation*.

---

**Subject:** Request for Human Review – Music Copyright Decision Regarding My Track “[Track Title]”

Dear [Platform Name] Copyright / Content ID / Legal Team,

I am writing regarding the decision made about my original track **[Track Title]**  
(ISRC: [ISRC if known] | Uploaded: [date] | URL: [link]).

I respectfully ask for written confirmation on this single binary question in line with the Burgess Principle:

**“Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific case before any decision was reached?”**

The specific facts of my case are:

• The track is an original composition and sound recording created by me [or co-created with named collaborators].  
• Any samples or interpolations used have been properly cleared and licensed [or the track contains no samples and is 100% original].  
• The automated match or claim appears to be a false positive because [brief factual reason, e.g. “the melody, rhythm, arrangement and sonic fingerprint are substantially different”].  
• This decision is causing ongoing harm: [e.g. monetisation blocked, track muted/removed, royalties withheld, distribution halted].

I am not challenging copyright law itself. I am simply requesting that a real human reviewer with musical and legal judgment has personally examined the specific facts of my track and claim, rather than relying solely on automated audio fingerprinting or checklist-based systems.

I would be grateful for a response within 7 days confirming:

1. That a named human reviewer has personally reviewed my specific case.  
2. The outcome of that review and any action taken (removal of the claim, restoration of monetisation, correction of royalty allocation, reinstatement of the track, etc.).

Thank you for treating my creative work as an individual artistic piece rather than just another automated match in the system.
