# Automated Decision-Making Challenge

A standalone template for challenging automated decisions under Article 22 of the UK GDPR. Use this when you believe a decision affecting you was made by an automated system without meaningful human intervention.

This is the core question of the Burgess Principle: **was a human there?**

Based on the actual Article 22 challenges used in the HMPO, Trading 212, and Apple cases.

Send this by email where possible. Record the date — the organisation has **30 calendar days** to respond.

---

## Template

```
Subject: Article 22 UK GDPR — Automated Decision-Making Challenge

Dear Data Protection Officer,

[INSTITUTION NAME]

I am writing to exercise my rights under Article 22 of the UK General
Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) in connection with the following
decision:

[DESCRIPTION OF THE DECISION OR ACTION TAKEN AGAINST YOU]

This decision was made on or around [DATE OF DECISION].

MY RIGHTS UNDER ARTICLE 22

Article 22(1) UK GDPR provides that I have the right not to be subject
to a decision based solely on automated processing — including profiling
— which produces legal effects concerning me or similarly significantly
affects me.

I REQUIRE THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION:

1. CONFIRMATION OF AUTOMATED PROCESSING
   Please confirm whether the above decision was made wholly or partly
   by an automated system, algorithm, or automated process.

2. HUMAN INTERVENTION
   If you state that a human was involved in the decision, please provide:
   (a) The name and role of the individual who reviewed my specific case
   (b) The date on which that review took place
   (c) Evidence that the individual had the authority and competence to
       override the automated output
   (d) Evidence that the individual actually exercised independent
       judgment — not simply rubber-stamped the automated recommendation

   Note: A human who merely clicks "approve" on an automated output
   without independent review of the individual case does not constitute
   meaningful human intervention (see Recital 71, UK GDPR).

3. LOGIC OF THE AUTOMATED PROCESSING
   Under Article 15(1)(h) UK GDPR, please provide meaningful information
   about the logic involved in the automated decision-making, including:
   (a) The data inputs used
   (b) The criteria or rules applied
   (c) The weighting given to different factors
   (d) The output or score that led to the decision

4. REQUEST FOR HUMAN REVIEW
   If no meaningful human intervention occurred, I request a review of
   the decision by a qualified human being under Article 22(3) UK GDPR.
   I also request the opportunity to:
   (a) Express my point of view
   (b) Contest the decision

IDENTITY VERIFICATION
I confirm my identity as follows:
- Full name: [YOUR NAME]
- Date of birth: [YOUR DOB]
- Address: [YOUR ADDRESS]
- Email: [YOUR EMAIL]
- Account/Reference: [ACCOUNT/REFERENCE NUMBER]

I am happy to provide further identification if reasonably required.

DEADLINE
Under Article 12(3) UK GDPR, you are required to respond to this request
within 30 calendar days of receipt.

Yours faithfully,

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[YOUR EMAIL]
```

## Placeholder Fields

| Placeholder | What to enter |
|-------------|---------------|
| `[YOUR NAME]` | Your full legal name |
| `[YOUR ADDRESS]` | Your current postal address |
| `[YOUR DOB]` | Your date of birth |
| `[YOUR EMAIL]` | Your email address |
| `[INSTITUTION NAME]` | The organisation that made the decision |
| `[ACCOUNT/REFERENCE NUMBER]` | Any reference number associated with your account or the decision |
| `[DESCRIPTION OF THE DECISION OR ACTION TAKEN AGAINST YOU]` | A clear description of what happened — e.g. "the rejection of my passport application", "the referral of my account to enforcement", "the decision to increase my insurance premium" |
| `[DATE OF DECISION]` | The date the decision was made or communicated to you |

## Where This Applies

Article 22 challenges are relevant in many situations where institutions use automated systems to make decisions about individuals. Common examples include:

- **Credit scoring** — automated approval or rejection of credit applications
- **Insurance pricing** — algorithmic risk assessment and premium calculation
- **Benefits decisions** — automated eligibility determinations
- **Enforcement referrals** — automated systems deciding to send bailiffs or enforcement agents
- **Warrant applications** — bulk processing of warrant requests without individual judicial review
- **Passport issuance** — automated identity verification and decision-making
- **Algorithmic profiling** — decisions based on automated profiling of behaviour or risk

The key question in every case is the same: did a human being individually review your specific case and exercise independent judgment, or did an automated system make the decision?

---

## What Counts as "Meaningful Human Intervention"?

Not every human involvement counts. For human intervention to be meaningful, the person must:

1. **Have authority** — they must be empowered to change the outcome
2. **Have competence** — they must understand what they are reviewing
3. **Have actually reviewed the individual case** — not just a batch or a summary
4. **Have exercised independent judgment** — not simply confirmed the automated output

A customer service agent reading from a script is not meaningful intervention. A manager who clicks "approve" on 500 cases in an hour is not meaningful intervention. A magistrate who signs 200 warrants in a single session is not meaningful intervention.

## After Sending

- Record the date you sent the request.
- If they do not respond within 30 days, see the [Ombudsman/Regulator Complaint](TEMPLATE_OMBUDSMAN_REFERRAL.md) template.
- If they claim a human was involved but cannot name that person or evidence their review, that response itself is significant.
- Keep every response — it forms part of your evidential record.
