# Short-Form Video Scripts & Ideas

Hi 👋

Here are refreshed short-form video ideas for explaining the Burgess Principle in a way that matches the repo as it exists now.

Tone: calm, kind, clear, non-confrontational.  
Format: vertical 9:16 (1080 × 1920) for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

## Core message to keep across all videos

- Start with the human feeling first: being unseen, dismissed, or processed.
- Keep the principle central: one calm question.
- Point people to the easiest entry point first: **Iris** on the hosted site or **START_HERE.md**.
- Mention privacy carefully and accurately: **local-first**, **on-device by default in Sovereign Local Mode**, no overclaiming.
- If relevant, explain that vague replies like "subject to human oversight" are not a clear answer yet — they are still **AMBIGUOUS** until the institution answers directly.

### Video Idea 1 — "One Calm Question" (~30 seconds)

Hook (0–3s):  
"Have you ever felt like a system responded to you without really seeing you?"

Main message (3–24s):  
Show everyday visuals: a letter, a phone screen, a person pausing before replying.  
Voiceover:  
"The Burgess Principle is one calm question you can ask any institution:

'Was a human member of the team able to personally review the specific facts of my specific situation?'

It is simple, respectful, and surprisingly hard to dodge."

CTA (24–30s):  
"Start with Iris or pick a template. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #HumanReview #BeSeen

### Video Idea 2 — "Not Every Reply Is a Real Answer" (~35 seconds)

Hook (0–4s):  
"If they say 'subject to human oversight'… that still might not answer your question."

Main message (4–28s):  
Show a vague institutional email on screen, then highlight the missing direct answer.  
Voiceover:  
"The Burgess Principle now uses three plain-language classifications:

SOVEREIGN — a real human personally reviewed the facts.  
NULL — no individual human review happened.  
AMBIGUOUS — the reply uses vague wording and still avoids a direct answer.

You do not need to argue. You can just ask politely for a clear yes or no."

CTA (28–35s):  
"Use the follow-up templates or ask Iris to help you draft the next message."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #KnowYourRights #HumanFirst

### Video Idea 3 — "Start on Your Phone" (~30 seconds)

Hook (0–3s):  
"You can do this from your phone."

Main message (3–24s):  
Show the hosted site, then a phone-first claim flow.  
Voiceover:  
"The easiest way in is the Burgess Principle site with Iris built in.

If you want more privacy later, Sovereign Local Mode lets you keep your claim flow on your own hardware, with local-first storage and optional signed receipts.

Start simple. Go deeper only if you want to."

CTA (24–30s):  
"Open the site, tap in, and begin. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #IrisAI #LocalFirst

### Video Idea 4 — "This Is More Than a Template" (~40 seconds)

Hook (0–4s):  
"This is not just a letter template repo anymore."

Main message (4–32s):  
Show quick cuts: templates, case studies, Iris, local mode, phone flow.  
Voiceover:  
"The Burgess Principle still gives you free templates.

But now it also has Iris, a guided starting point, real case studies, local-first workflows, and an optional evidence trail if you need to keep careful records.

You can begin with one message today and build from there only if needed."

CTA (32–40s):  
"Start with START_HERE or talk to Iris. Link in bio."

Hashtags: #BurgessPrinciple #OpenSource #HelpfulTools

## Production tips

- Keep the delivery gentle and grounded.
- Use large captions and leave the one core question on screen long enough to read.
- Show real, ordinary contexts: bills, housing letters, phone screens, email drafts.
- Do not overstate legal effect or privacy claims.
- If you mention local mode, describe it as optional and local-first.
- If you mention results, point people toward the case studies and audit log rather than making sweeping promises.

Feel free to adapt these scripts to your own voice. The goal is to help more people discover a calm, practical way to ask for real human review.
